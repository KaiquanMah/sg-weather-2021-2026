### **Monthly/Quarterly Trends in 2025 (Refer to Images 2A, 3A, 4A, 5A):**

**HUMIDITY:**
- **Highest:** November to December (85.92% to 86.35%)
- **Lowest:** July (81.38%)
  - At 81.38% humidity in July (the lowest in the year), Singapore is still very humid, compared to other places in the world
- **Pattern:** U-shaped curve
  - Higher humidity around the start and end of the year
  - Lower humidity in the middle of the year (Q2 and Q3, from May to October)

**RAINFALL:**
- **Highest:** October to November (0.017mm to 0.018mm monthly avg)

- **Lowest:** June to September (0.004mm to 0.005mm)
- **Q4:** Wettest quarter (0.013mm)

**WIND SPEED:**
- **Windiest:** January to February (4.73 to 4.78 knots) - Northeast Monsoon
- **Calmest:** November (3.06 knots), April to June (3.16 to 3.39 knots)

**TEMPERATURE:**
- **Hottest:** May (28.24°C)

## **OVERALL INSIGHTS FOR 2025:**

### **1. Urban Area Heating Effect:**
Marina South (CBD area) is **2°C hotter** than the national average (29.70°C vs ~27.85°C) from dense buildings and heavy vehicle traffic.

### **2. Monsoon-Driven Climate:**
- **Northeast Monsoon (December to Early March):** Windy, cooler, wetter
- **Inter-monsoon (April to May):** Calm winds, intense heating
- **Southwest Monsoon (June to September):** Moderate conditions
- **Northeast Monsoon transition (October to November):** Highest humidity and rainfall
- These 4 periods are based on the [Singapore's climate patterns](https://www.weather.gov.sg/climate-climate-of-singapore/)

### **3. Geographic Extremes:**
- **Northern regions:** Highest humidity
- **Central urban area (Marina South):** Higher temperature, lower humidity
- **Coastal areas (Western Islands, Tuas):** Higher rainfall and humidity

### **4. Seasonal Inverse Relationships:**
- **Wind vs Temperature:** Windy Q1 = cooler; Calm Q2 = hottest
- **Rainfall vs Wind:** Wettest months (October to November) don't coincide with windiest (January to February)
